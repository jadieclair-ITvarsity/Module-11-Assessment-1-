Graphite

  • Graphite Sticks: For broad strokes and covering large areas quickly.
  • Pencil Techniques: Similar to pencil drawing, using hatching, cross-hatching, and blending.
  • Powdered Graphite: For smooth, even shading over large areas using a brush or cloth.

Pencil

  • Hatching and Cross-Hatching: Creating tonal or shading effects with closely spaced parallel lines or intersecting lines.
  • Blending: Using tools like blending stumps or tissues to smooth out graphite and create gradients.
  • Stippling: Creating texture and shading with small dots.

Charcoal

  • Vine Charcoal: For light sketches and outlines, easily erasable.
  • Compressed Charcoal: For darker lines and more intense shading.
  • Lifting: Using an eraser to lift charcoal from the paper, creating highlights and lighter areas.
